⚙️ Group Session Dossier

Everything each role needs to run a High Ticket sales role-play as a team. Simple, to the point, always at hand.

1 The 4 roles

🎯 Facilitator / Trainer

Runs the session: generates the lead, controls the stage timer, sees the Private Info (real objection and root causes), fires surprise events and leads the debrief.

📈 Closer

The one practicing the sale. They only see the product and the lead's name — everything else is discovered by asking. The call gets scored and earns fictional commission.

🎭 Lead

Plays the prospect with their character sheet: who they are, their pain, their visible objection and how to talk. The mission is realistic friction, not over-cooperating.

👁 Observer

Watches the "match" and captures the truth: 1-5 rubric (rapport, real pain, value gap, recap, isolation) and the listening log (key quotes, decisive moment).

2 Session flow

  1. Create the roomThe Trainer creates the room and shares the ID. Everyone joins with their role (phones work too, as Lead or Observer).
  2. Generate the leadIndustry, difficulty (Beginner/Intermediate/Advanced), temperature (Cold/Warm/Hot) and objection — or the business's real product. The AI builds identity, psychology, backstory and DISC personality.
  3. Live role-playCloser vs. Lead through the 6 pipeline stages, with a per-stage timer. The Trainer can fire a surprise event; the team can open votes.
  4. Observer captureRubric + listening log during the call. It's direct human observation — the final analysis weighs it heavily.
  5. Analysis & commissionThe AI analyzes everything with the methodology rubric → 1-10 score, fictional commission, streak and global/monthly leaderboard.

3 The objection onion

Smokescreen What the lead SAYS first: "I need to think about it", "it's too expensive"…

Real objection The guarded reason that only surfaces once the closer earns trust.

🧅 Underneath there are 1 to 3 layered root causes — [Wound] they got scammed before, [Fear] of failing again, [Identity] "I'm not the kind who pulls this off". The lead reveals them one at a time, as trust grows. The Trainer sees them in Private Info; the final analysis measures how deep the closer got.

4 The consultative pipeline (6 stages)

1
Opening / RapportConnect before convincing. Signal: their guard drops.
2
QualificationDiagnose real pain, desire and urgency. Signal: they admit emotional burnout.
3
Opportunity CostThe price of staying the same. Signal: explicit urgency.
4
RecapOrder their mental chaos. Signal: "that's exactly what's happening to me".
5
PresentationThe vehicle's map anchored to their emotion. Signal: they picture themselves inside.
6
CloseMicro-close, investment and objections from identity (not haggling).

5 Scoring & commissions

Close → commissionProduct price × configured % (10% by default).
No close → creditPractice still pays based on the score.
DifficultyBeginner ×0.8 · Intermediate ×1.0 · Advanced ×1.4
TemperatureCold ×1.15 · Warm ×1.0 · Hot ×0.9
Team spiritHelped as Lead/Observer this week: +10%. Over a week without helping: −15%.
LeaderboardAll-time global + monthly tournament that resets to zero.

6 Quick tools per role

  • Trainer: Private Info (real objection + root causes), regenerate scenario, scenario library, surprise event, cohorts and student analytics.
  • Lead: character sheet (how you talk, what opens you up, what shuts you down) + backup objection arsenal.
  • Observer: 1-5 rubric, active-listening log and team votes.
  • Closer: pipeline with lifesaver questions per stage and the product to sell always visible.
