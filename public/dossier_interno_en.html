🔌 Technical Setup (Facilitator)

The control panel allows you to orchestrate the AI simulation with surgical precision.

👇 (Swipe down)

Scenario & Context

  • Product/Price: Define what is sold and its value. This calibrates the difficulty of the close.
  • Lead's Context: Create information asymmetry. Give the Lead (Human or AI) a realistic background (industry, company size, hidden pains).
  • Initial Objections: Force the Lead to throw specific smokescreens ("It's expensive", "I have no time") to evaluate the Closer's reaction.

AI Integration (API)

  • API Key & URL: Sales Arena supports any endpoint compatible with OpenAI. Data is saved locally.
  • Cloud Models: By default, it uses meta/llama-3.1-8b-instruct via Nvidia, but you can inject OpenAI keys or other providers.
  • Ollama (Local): To run local models without latency, use http://localhost:11434/v1. ⚠️ Vital: Start Ollama with OLLAMA_ORIGINS="*" to prevent CORS blocking in the browser.

📊 The Consultative Pipeline

The 6-stage methodology every Closer must follow and every Observer must evaluate.

👇 (Swipe down)

Stages 1 to 3: The Diagnosis

  1. 1. Opening / Rapport: Connect before convincing. Validate without condescension.
    Indicator: The lead lowers their guard.
  2. 2. Qualification: Diagnose using the checklist: Real reason, Origin, Present Pain, Time Gap, and Trust.
    Indicator: The lead admits emotional exhaustion.
  3. 3. Opportunity Cost: Make visible the price of taking no action. "How much do you lose per month by staying like this?".
    Indicator: Explicit urgency.

Stages 4 to 6: The Close

  1. 4. Recapitulation: Organize the lead's mental chaos by summarizing their pains and goals to generate dissonance.
    Indicator: The lead says "That is exactly what happens to me".
  2. 5. Presentation: Show the logical map of the vehicle anchored to emotion. Explain program phases.
    Indicator: Visualizes success with your product.
  3. 6. Close: Intention micro-close, investment reveal, and handling objections from Identity (not haggling).

🎭 Tools for the LEAD

Your goal is to simulate realistic friction. Use these guidelines to play the prospect:

Awareness Level

  • Unaware (Doesn't know they have a problem).
  • Problem Aware (But not aware of your solution).

Personality Archetypes

  • The Skeptic: Short answers, demands proof.
  • The Rambler: Talks a lot, avoids the point.

The Game of Toughness

Start with "smokescreen" (superficial) objections. Only reveal your real pain if the Closer manages to build rapport and asks the right questions. If the Closer seems to be reading a script, get defensive.

🕵️‍♂️ Tools for the OBSERVER

You are not a spectator, you are the quality control analyst.

5-Point Operational Checklist:

  • 1. Rapport Level: Did they build rapport before interrogating? Did they validate emotions?
  • 2. Real vs Superficial Pain: Did they stay on the symptom or dig into the real cause of frustration?
  • 3. Value Gap: Did they make the Lead express their 'Opportunity Cost'?
  • 4. Recapitulation: Did they organize the Lead's chaos and summarize the situation before pitching?
  • 5. Objection Isolation: Did they try to convince with logic at the first excuse, or did they dig to find the hidden objection?
